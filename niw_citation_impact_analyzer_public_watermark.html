NIW Citation Impact Analyzer

This public-facing tool is designed to help a researcher reconsider a publication record from an NIW perspective. It does not provide a legal conclusion, does not predict approval, and does not disclose this office's internal adjudication methodology. Instead, it organizes the record into research trajectory, citation quality, development pattern, and public-facing analytical observations.

Public version only. Internal scoring logic, weighting, and adjudication thresholds are intentionally withheld.

2. Why This Tool Exists

The question is not whether a citation number is “high enough.” The question is what the publication record actually says about the applicant’s research influence.

This framework is intended to move beyond a raw publication list and toward a more coherent view of research impact. It asks whether the publications form a trajectory, whether the current research grows out of that trajectory, and whether outside researchers, institutions, or technical users have engaged meaningfully with the work.
In NIW analysis, the issue is not merely how many papers exist or how many citations appear. The more important question is whether the research can be understood as a meaningful and developing body of work.

Generated Analysis

This output is designed to read like a public-facing analytical memorandum rather than a scoring calculator or legal decision tool.

After entering the research and publication record, click Generate Analysis. The tool will produce a narrative assessment showing how the record may be understood from an NIW perspective, including indicators of influence, issues requiring caution, and framing points for legal analysis.
